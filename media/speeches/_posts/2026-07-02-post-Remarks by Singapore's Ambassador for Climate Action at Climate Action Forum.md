<p><strong>OPENING REMARKS BY SINGAPORE'S AMBASSADOR FOR CLIMATE ACTION&nbsp;AT CLIMATE ACTION FORUM, SINGAPORE</strong>
</p>
<hr>
<p>Minister Grace Fu, Mr Loh Boon Chye, ladies and gentlemen, good morning.</p>
<p>I just got back from London Climate Action Week. I want to share three
things I saw.</p>
<p><strong><em>First, global climate action is not slowing down.</em></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>London Climate Week was at least five times bigger than our own highly
successful Ecosperity Week.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Corporates, financiers, and policymakers from around the world were there
— not to make grand speeches, but to draw up concrete initiatives:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>on sustainability reporting, transition planning, carbon markets, climate
finance.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><em>Second, the demands on companies are rising.</em></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Investors, customers, and suppliers are all incorporating sustainability
into their decisions.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism is on track – and it requires
exporters to report the carbon content of their goods.</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>This makes climate disclosure a trade requirement, not just a capital
markets one.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><em>Third, climate risks are not distant.</em></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>London was hit by an unprecedented heat wave while we were there.</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Schools closed, hospitals were overwhelmed.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Some trains and buses stopped — because tracks overheated, roads buckled.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Climate change is not a forecast but a current reality: one which will
only get worse.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><em>How do our companies prepare for this?</em>
</p>
<p><strong><em>The first step is to understand that climate reporting is not compliance, it is competitiveness.</em></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Climate reporting is the foundation of all climate action</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>You cannot manage what you do not measure.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>You cannot build a transition plan without emissions data.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>And without a credible transition plan, you cannot access the capital
and customers that now demand one.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><em>But companies face real barriers. Data is hard to get. Requirements are complex.</em>
</p>
<p><strong><em>Singapore has two advantages that enable us to do this better than most.</em></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>One, we are good at digital technology: to automate data collection, reduce
reporting burdens, and embed AI-powered guidance.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Two, we are good at ecosystem integration: to build common infrastructure
to link climate reporting to transition planning and supply chain engagement.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><em>Today, leveraging on these two capabilities, we are taking our sustainability reporting infrastructure to the next stage.</em>
</p>
<p><strong><em>First, enhance ESGenome with ISSB-focused modules and AI-powered capabilities.</em></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>ESGenome — launched by SGX and MAS in 2022 — has already helped over 200
listed companies with their sustainability reporting.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Today, through a new partnership between SGX and Gprnt, ESGenome is being
substantially upgraded.</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>New modules will help companies meet their mandatory ISSB-based climate
disclosure obligations.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Scope 1 and 2 calculations will be automated using trusted government
data.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>And AI-powered features for transition planning are on the way.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><em>Second, provide access to climate data for transition planning.</em></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>SGX Group and Gprnt will work with the C3T — the Council for a Competitive
Climate Transition — to give listed companies the information they need
on climate risks, transition pathways, and sectoral benchmarks.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The goal: disclosures that inform strategy, not merely satisfy regulators.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><em>Third, enable the greening of supply chains.</em></strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>SGX-listed companies are the natural Queen Bees for C3T’s Green 100 initiative
which aims to bring along SME suppliers on the sustainability reporting
journey.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>By doing so, you strengthen their resilience, improve your Scope 3 data,
and enable greener procurement.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>In short, climate reporting becomes an ecosystem effort. No Singapore
company needs to walk alone.</p>
<p>And when we walk together, we build a Singapore economy that is resilient
and competitive in a climate-impaired and carbon-constrained world.</p>
